Week 1: QA Testing & Design

February 20, 23-28 2026

Quality Assurance Training & CCPOS Testing

The week started with a focus on Quality Assurance. On February 20th, I spent the morning studying Software Quality Assurance principles through resources provided by my mentor. By 5:00 PM, I was ready to apply these concepts by testing the CCPOS (Cynthia Curry Point of Sale) system. This first hands-on experience was crucial in understanding what it takes to be a thorough QA tester.

Testing Report & Documentation

On Monday (Feb 23rd), I spent the entire day reviewing the CCPOS system and documenting all the issues I found. From 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM, I meticulously created detailed report notes covering every bug and issue I discovered. The commitment didn't end there – I stayed until 7:00 PM to finalize and submit the comprehensive report. This taught me the importance of thoroughness and attention to detail in QA work.

Site Inspection & Flow Analysis

On Tuesday (Feb 24th), the project shifted from testing to design. Our team conducted an on-site visit to the restaurant to understand the actual workflow. We inspected the restaurant's layout, visited each room, examined the kitchen area, and documented the number of tables in each room. Through discussions with the restaurant staff, I gained valuable insights into their current system's flow and identified key pain points. This visit was essential for understanding real-world requirements before designing a solution.

Flowchart & Initial Documentation

Based on all the information gathered, we brainstormed and discussed the ideal flow for the new POS system. I helped create a comprehensive flowchart that visualized how the system should work, incorporating feedback from the restaurant staff and addressing the issues we identified in their current system.

UI Design & Database Architecture

Wednesday (Feb 25th) marked the beginning of the design phase. I started developing prototype designs in Figma and began drafting the Entity-Relationship Diagram (ERD) for the database. This continued on Thursday (Feb 26th) with further refinement of the UI design and detailed ERD development. Through discussions with the team, we ensured that both the user interface and database design would support all the features the restaurant needed.

Mentor Review & Stakeholder Presentation

On Friday (Feb 27th), my mentor reviewed the UI designs and we discussed the specific features the restaurant owner wanted. Based on this feedback, we scheduled a meeting with the restaurant staff and owner for a presentation of the UI and to gather their feedback. Additionally, over the weekend, I continued refining the UI design for both the admin and owner dashboards to ensure all stakeholders' needs were met.

Key Learnings from Week 1

  • Importance of thorough testing and detailed documentation in QA
  • Value of understanding real-world user workflows before designing
  • How to gather and incorporate stakeholder feedback into design
  • The process of translating business requirements into technical designs (Flowchart & ERD)
  • Balancing UI/UX design with database architecture considerations

Week 1 Reflection

This first week was incredibly productive and educational. I went from learning QA principles to actively designing a real POS system for a restaurant. The combination of practical testing experience, site visits, design work, and stakeholder collaboration gave me a comprehensive understanding of the full development lifecycle. I'm excited to see how this system will evolve in the coming weeks and to contribute further to its development.
