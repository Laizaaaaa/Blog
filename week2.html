Week 2: UI Presentation & Feedback

March 1-7 2026

6 min read

UI Presentation & Stakeholder Feedback

Week two was focused on presenting our design to the stakeholders and incorporating their feedback. On Monday (March 2nd), we presented the UI and ordering flow to Sir George, Sir James, and Sir Eric. This was a crucial moment – seeing their reactions to our designs and hearing their suggestions shaped the direction of the entire project. They provided valuable feedback on the table layout, emphasizing the importance of including customer demographics (age and gender) and peak time sales analytics in the dashboard.

Multi-System Integration Discussion

On Tuesday (March 3rd), we continued working on the revised UI based on their feedback. During our meeting with Sir George, we discovered the importance of user roles and access control. He introduced a big vision of integrating three interconnected systems: a POS system, an accounting system, and an inventory management system. Together, we discussed the different user roles, management structures, and how these systems would communicate with each other. This expanded scope elevated the project to a new level of complexity.

Understanding Business Processes

Wednesday (March 4th) was dedicated to understanding the accounting processes. We had detailed discussions with Sir Eric about how accounting would work within our system. We also participated in a discussion with both Sir Eric and Sir George about what components would be included in the accounting module. Their insights directly influenced our UI modifications, as we needed to design interfaces that would facilitate these complex business processes. Between these meetings, we even helped Sir James paint a diorama during our afternoon break – a nice bonding moment with the team.

Refining the Vision with Mentorship

During the overtime session with Sir Ace, our mentor, we asked critical questions about the system architecture and approach. His advice was practical: focus on perfecting the POS system first, and handle the accounting and inventory integration later. This guidance helped us avoid trying to do too much at once and maintain focus on what was most important.

Design Finalization & Approval

On Thursday (March 5th), we continued refining the UI design based on all the feedback we had gathered. By Friday (March 6th), we finalized the UI design and presented it once more to Sir George for approval. After making some last-minute adjustments he requested, we received the green light to begin development the following week. This approval was the result of our teamwork and continuous improvement.

Key Learnings from Week 2

  • The importance of stakeholder feedback in shaping project direction
  • How to balance big vision with practical focus
  • Understanding business processes before implementing technical solutions
  • Value of mentorship in making strategic decisions
  • Iterative design improvement leads to better final products
  • Building relationships with stakeholders and team members

Week 2 Reflection

This week transformed my understanding of the project. What started as a simple POS system evolved into a comprehensive, multi-system solution. The feedback and discussions exposed me to real business requirements and how technology must adapt to serve those needs. Getting approval to move into development was exciting, and I felt confident that we had built a solid foundation through this collaborative process.
